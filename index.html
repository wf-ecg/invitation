EXPERIENCE COMMUNITY


With your help, the Wells Fargo Championship has donated over $16 million to charity.

Reading Above Par® is a community program by Wells Fargo to support the importance of childhood reading literacy. The program was expanded last year to include a reading program with The First Tee of Charlotte. During the month of April, Wells Fargo Volunteers worked directly with students enrolled in The First Tee of Charlotte, meeting weekly to read together with the goal of completing a golf related book that reinforced character development in advance of tournament week. The program culminated on tournament week when Wells Fargo sent company leaders and PGA TOUR players Lucas Glover, Rickie Fowler, JJ Henry, and Scott Langley to The First Tee of Charlotte. Each PGA TOUR player read a page from “P is For Putt: A Golf Alphabet” that corresponds to the first letter of their name and also spent time speaking to students about the importance of education and reading. Afterwards the PGA TOUR players provided an interactive golf skills session for The First Tee of Charlotte students.


Reading Above Par®       Readers in 5,000 classrooms       Over 1 million books donated.

Succeeding TogetherTM is a video essay contest created by Wells Fargo for teenage participants of The First Tee who want to share examples of how working together has shaped their lives so far. Beginning in March, interested candidates were asked to submit a short video of themselves addressing several questions. The videos submitted were voted on by the public, and then four videos were selected as finalists. The grand prize winner was awarded a trip to Charlotte to attend the Wells Fargo Championship and had the unique opportunity to play in the Wednesday ProAm with defending champion, Rickie Fowler. The other three runner-ups received a four-day, three-night trip to Charlotte to attend the Wells Fargo Championship and experience the event from inside the ropes as a guest of Wells Fargo.
       The winner of last year’s contest was Reyhan Griffin (pictured), a 14-year-old member of The First Tee of San Francisco. As an eighth-grade student, Reyhan carries a 4.0 GPA and practices golf six days a week. His video essay cites integrity and golf as his two favorite core values of The First Tee program.

TENDING THE FLAG ON 17

The standard Wells Fargo Championship pin flag on Quail Hollow Club’s signature 17th hole was replaced this year with an American Flag. Each day, Thursday through Sunday, a different Wells Fargo Team Member who is an active reservist or military veteran was asked to tend the flag. This marks the first year Wells Fargo has adopted this program.
